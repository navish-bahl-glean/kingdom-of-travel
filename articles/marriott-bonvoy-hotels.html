10 Marriott Bonvoy Redemptions Under 35,000 Points Per Night

Updated: May 2025 11 min read By KingdomOfTravel Editorial

Marriott Bonvoy's dynamic pricing model has made sweet spots harder to find than they were under the old category system — but they absolutely still exist. Knowing where to look is the difference between a 15,000-point night at a forgettable property and a 28,000-point night at a stunning boutique hotel in Lisbon or a beachfront resort in Thailand.

👑 How We Picked These

We prioritized properties where the cash rate frequently exceeds $250 CAD per night — meaning you're getting at minimum 0.7 cents per point in value, and often 1.0+ cents. All properties listed have been verified to regularly price under 35,000 points during shoulder season.

How Marriott Bonvoy Dynamic Pricing Works in 2025

Marriott eliminated fixed award categories in 2022, replacing them with dynamic pricing that fluctuates based on cash demand. However, most properties have a soft floor and ceiling — a budget Courtyard will rarely exceed 20,000 points even at peak times, while a top-tier St. Regis or Edition may still price at 85,000+ points. The sweet spots in this list sit in the middle: genuine upscale or premium properties that haven't been "re-priced" to match their cash rates.

1. The Westin Excelsior, Rome

One of Rome's grand historic hotels, steps from the Via Veneto and a short walk to the Villa Borghese gardens. Cash rates routinely hit €350–€500/night (CAD $520–$750). In shoulder season (November–February, excluding holidays), award rates regularly dip to 28,000–32,000 points per night.

  • Points: 28,000–35,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $520–$750 CAD/night
  • Best months: November, January, February

2. Le Méridien Piccadilly, London

A classic London address on Piccadilly, with views over St. James's. London hotel prices are notoriously high — weeknight cash rates here average £280–£380. Award availability at 25,000–34,000 points shows up regularly midweek outside of peak summer, making this one of the best pure-value Bonvoy redemptions in a major European city.

  • Points: 25,000–34,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $480–$700 CAD/night
  • Best months: October through March (excluding Christmas)

3. The Westin Siray Bay Resort, Phuket

A secluded resort on Siray Bay with a stunning infinity pool overlooking the Andaman Sea. Cash rates during the high season (December–March) hit $400–$600 CAD/night. In shoulder season, this property consistently awards at 22,000–30,000 points per night — exceptional value for a full-scale beach resort.

  • Points: 22,000–30,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $300–$600 CAD/night
  • Best months: April–May, September–October

4. Sheraton Lisboa Hotel & Spa, Lisbon

Lisbon has become one of Europe's most-visited cities, and hotel prices have followed. The Sheraton Lisboa sits in the Marquês de Pombal neighbourhood with panoramic city views. Peak season cash rates reach €280–€400/night. Award rates of 20,000–28,000 points are common outside summer, making this one of the clearest value plays in Southern Europe.

  • Points: 20,000–28,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $420–$600 CAD/night
  • Best months: November through March

5. The Naka Island, a Luxury Collection Resort (Phuket)

A private island resort accessible only by speedboat, The Naka Island is one of Southeast Asia's most romantic properties. It's a Luxury Collection property — the same tier as some of Marriott's most expensive hotels globally — yet it prices at just 30,000–35,000 points per night in shoulder season. Cash rates hit $700–$1,200 CAD/night during high season.

  • Points: 30,000–35,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $600–$1,200 CAD/night
  • Best months: May, June, September, October
💡 The 5th Night Free Trick

Marriott Bonvoy offers a 5th night free on award stays of 5 consecutive nights. If you're staying 5 nights, you pay only 4. This effectively reduces the per-night cost by 20% and can make even higher-priced properties competitive.

6. W Amsterdam

The W brand's Amsterdam outpost occupies two converted 17th-century canal houses in the heart of the city. Stylish, central, and an experience in itself — cash rates hit €350–€500 regularly. Off-peak award pricing of 28,000–33,000 points makes it one of the better urban hotel redemptions in Europe.

  • Points: 28,000–33,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $520–$750 CAD/night
  • Best months: November through February

7. Sheraton Grand Macao Hotel

One of the largest hotels in the world, but don't let the scale put you off — the Sheraton Grand Macao features excellent rooms, multiple pools, and is connected to the Cotai Strip casinos. For travellers routing through Asia, award rates of 15,000–25,000 points per night are common, with cash rates frequently exceeding $350 CAD.

  • Points: 15,000–25,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $350–$500 CAD/night
  • Best for: Asia stopovers, gaming enthusiasts

8. The Westin Dragonara Resort, Malta

Malta remains undervisited relative to its beauty, and the Westin Dragonara sits dramatically on a peninsula jutting into the Mediterranean. Award rates of 25,000–32,000 points in shoulder season are well below the $500–$800 CAD cash rate. For travellers combining Malta with Sicily or southern Italy, this is a natural home base.

  • Points: 25,000–32,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $500–$800 CAD/night
  • Best months: October–November, April–May

9. Marriott Resort Momi Bay, Fiji

Fiji on points is a dream, and the Marriott Resort Momi Bay delivers a full overwater-bungalow-style experience at a fraction of the cash price. Award rates regularly appear at 30,000–35,000 points per night — while cash rates for comparable rooms hit $600–$900 CAD. For Canadians, this is one of the best long-haul resort redemptions available.

  • Points: 30,000–35,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $600–$900 CAD/night
  • Best months: June through September (Fiji's dry season)

10. AC Hotel by Marriott, Barcelona Forum

Barcelona's sleek, design-forward AC brand property near the Forum district. Less central than some options but steps from the beach and significantly cheaper in points. Award rates of 18,000–28,000 points are common, with cash rates of €200–€350 — making this one of the best entry-level Bonvoy redemptions in Spain.

  • Points: 18,000–28,000/night
  • Cash equivalent: $300–$520 CAD/night
  • Best months: November through March

How to Earn Marriott Bonvoy Points as a Canadian

The fastest routes to Bonvoy points for Canadians:

  • Amex MR Transfer: Convert Amex Membership Rewards to Bonvoy at 1:1.2. The Amex Cobalt's 5x on dining is the fastest way to pile up points.
  • Marriott Bonvoy American Express Card: Canada's only co-branded Bonvoy card. Welcome bonus of 50,000–70,000 points; earns 5x at Marriott properties.
  • Staying at Marriott properties: Elite status earns bonus points; Titanium Elite earns 75% bonus on base points.
