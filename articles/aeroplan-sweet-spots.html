The Best Aeroplan Sweet Spots for Business Class in 2025

Updated: May 2025 12 min read By KingdomOfTravel Editorial

Aeroplan is one of the most flexible points currencies in Canada — and in 2025, the program's distance-based chart still hides some remarkable business class sweet spots that most travellers overlook. Whether you're chasing Air Canada's Signature Suite or booking partner carriers like Lufthansa and ANA, here are the redemptions delivering the most value per point.

👑 Key Takeaway

The best Aeroplan business class value comes from transatlantic routes on Star Alliance partners — especially Lufthansa, Swiss, and Austrian — where you can book long-haul business class at the same rate as Air Canada but with superior hard products.

How Aeroplan Pricing Works

Since Aeroplan switched to a dynamic-ish pricing model in 2021, award prices are no longer published in a simple chart. However, pricing is largely distance-based for partner redemptions, and Air Canada's own flights still follow predictable bands. The sweet spots below represent routes where the point cost is systematically low relative to the cash price.

Sweet Spot #1 — Canada to Europe in Business Class

Route: Toronto/Montreal/Vancouver → London, Frankfurt, Munich, Zurich

This remains Aeroplan's single best long-haul business class sweet spot. Flights from Canada to continental Europe on Lufthansa, Swiss, or Austrian typically price at 75,000–85,000 Aeroplan points one-way in business class. The same flight in cash frequently costs $4,000–$6,000 CAD.

  • Points required: 75,000–85,000 one-way in business
  • Cash equivalent: $4,000–$6,000 CAD
  • Value per point: 5–8 cents
  • Best partners: Lufthansa (Business), Swiss (Business), Austrian (Business)

Lufthansa's long-haul business class features a fully flat bed, direct aisle access, and one of the best catering programs in the sky. Booking Lufthansa through Aeroplan also avoids Lufthansa's own punishing fuel surcharges — you pay Aeroplan's carrier surcharge instead, which is significantly lower.

Sweet Spot #2 — Canada to Japan on ANA

Route: Vancouver/Toronto → Tokyo (Narita or Haneda)

ANA's business class — "The Room" on select 777 and 787 routes — is widely considered the best business class product in the world on certain aircraft configurations. Vancouver to Tokyo prices at approximately 75,000–90,000 Aeroplan points one-way in business class. The cash price routinely exceeds $5,000 CAD.

  • Points required: 75,000–90,000 one-way in business
  • Cash equivalent: $5,000–$7,000 CAD
  • Value per point: 6–9 cents
  • Best for: Travellers who want the absolute best hard product on transpacific routes
💡 Booking Tip

ANA business class availability opens 355 days in advance. Search aeroplan.com starting exactly 355 days before your desired travel date. Peak summer routes fill within hours of opening.

Sweet Spot #3 — Air Canada Signature Suite (Transcontinental)

Route: Toronto/Montreal → Vancouver/Calgary

Air Canada's Signature Class on domestic transcontinental routes (YYZ/YUL to YVR/YYC) is wildly underpriced in points. These seats — lie-flat beds on narrow-body aircraft — often price at just 25,000–35,000 Aeroplan points one-way, while cash prices can reach $800–$1,500 CAD. For a 4–5 hour domestic flight with a lie-flat bed and chef-inspired meals, this is outstanding value.

  • Points required: 25,000–35,000 one-way
  • Cash equivalent: $800–$1,500 CAD
  • Value per point: 3–5 cents
  • Best for: Domestic travellers wanting a premium experience

Sweet Spot #4 — Canada to Southeast Asia via Star Alliance

Route: Vancouver → Bangkok, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur

Routing through Tokyo or Hong Kong on Star Alliance partners can unlock premium cabin seats to Southeast Asia at excellent rates. Thai Airways business class to Bangkok from Vancouver often prices around 90,000–105,000 points one-way — extraordinary value for a flight that frequently costs $5,000–$8,000 CAD in cash.

  • Points required: 90,000–105,000 one-way
  • Cash equivalent: $5,000–$8,000 CAD
  • Value per point: 5–8 cents

How to Earn Enough Aeroplan Points

A business class transatlantic redemption requires 75,000–90,000 points. Here's how to hit that target:

  • TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite welcome bonus: up to 50,000 points
  • CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite welcome bonus: up to 40,000 points (hold both)
  • Amex MR transfer: Convert Amex Membership Rewards to Aeroplan at 1:1
  • Flying Air Canada: Earn base miles plus elite bonus miles

A common strategy: open both the TD and CIBC Aeroplan cards in the same year. Between welcome bonuses alone, you'll have 90,000 points — enough for one business class transatlantic flight without a single dollar of everyday spending.

Taxes and Fees to Expect

Even on points redemptions, you'll pay carrier surcharges and government taxes. On Air Canada–operated transatlantic flights, expect to pay $150–$300 CAD in fees per person. On Lufthansa and Swiss, Aeroplan passes through lower surcharges than booking directly — typically $200–$400 CAD versus $700–$1,000 CAD when booking on lufthansa.com.
