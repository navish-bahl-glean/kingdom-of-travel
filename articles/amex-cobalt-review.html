Amex Cobalt Review: Still Canada's Best Everyday Earner in 2025

Updated: May 2025 10 min read By KingdomOfTravel Editorial

The American Express Cobalt Card has held the top spot in our Canadian credit card rankings since 2019, and in 2025 it still hasn't been dethroned. No other Canadian card earns five points per dollar on food and drink purchases — and for most Canadians, food and drink represent their single largest spending category outside of housing.

👑 Our Verdict

The Amex Cobalt is the single best first travel credit card for Canadians. If you only ever carry one rewards card, this is it. The 5x earn rate on groceries and dining alone justifies the monthly fee for virtually every cardholder.

Amex Cobalt at a Glance

  • Annual fee: $12.99/month ($155.88/year)
  • Welcome bonus: Up to 15,000 MR points (1,250 pts/month for 12 months, upon $500 monthly spend)
  • Earn rate: 5x on food & drinks, 3x on streaming, 2x on travel & transit, 1x everything else
  • Points currency: American Express Membership Rewards
  • Key transfer partners: Aeroplan (1:1), Marriott Bonvoy (1:1.2), Avios (1:1)
  • Insurance: Travel accident, flight delay, baggage delay, hotel burglary

The 5x Earn Rate — Why It Matters

Five points per dollar sounds great in theory, but what does it actually mean in practice? If you spend $1,500/month on groceries and dining — a reasonable figure for a Canadian household — you're earning 7,500 MR points every single month just from food. At a conservative 2 cents per point valuation (Aeroplan redemptions regularly deliver 3–5 cents), that's $150/month in travel value from food spending alone.

The annual fee is $155.88. You're essentially getting the card for free after just one month of grocery and dining spend. Every other month is pure profit.

What Counts as "Food & Drinks" for 5x

American Express uses merchant category codes (MCCs) to determine the 5x category. In practice, this is very broad and includes:

  • Grocery stores (Loblaws, Metro, Sobeys, Whole Foods, Costco)
  • Restaurants and fast food chains
  • Coffee shops (Starbucks, Tim Hortons, Second Cup)
  • Food delivery apps (DoorDash, Uber Eats, SkipTheDishes)
  • Bars and nightclubs
  • Bakeries and specialty food stores

Notable exclusions: Walmart and Costco gas bars typically code as general merchandise or fuel rather than grocery, which earns 1x. Prepared food at Costco's food court does earn 5x at most locations.

Membership Rewards Flexibility

The real power of the Cobalt is that MR points are the most flexible points currency in Canada. Unlike Aeroplan miles (which are best used for Air Canada and partners) or Marriott Bonvoy points (hotel-focused), MR points can be transferred to multiple programs:

  • Aeroplan: 1,000 MR = 1,000 Aeroplan miles. This is the most popular transfer for flight redemptions.
  • Marriott Bonvoy: 1,000 MR = 1,200 Bonvoy points. Useful for hotel redemptions.
  • British Airways Avios: 1,000 MR = 1,000 Avios. Best for short-haul business class on BA and Iberia.
  • Fixed travel credit: Redeem MR points at 1 cent each toward any travel booking. Not our recommended use, but a reliable floor value.

The Welcome Bonus: How to Maximize It

The Cobalt's welcome bonus is structured differently from most cards. Instead of a lump sum after a minimum spend, you earn 1,250 bonus MR points each month for 12 months when you spend at least $500 in that month. This means:

  • Total bonus potential: 15,000 MR points over 12 months
  • Monthly spend required: $500 (very achievable — groceries alone often cover this)
  • Value of bonus at 2 cents/pt: $300
  • Value of bonus at Aeroplan rates (3–5 cents/pt): $450–$750
💡 Pro Tip

Pair the Cobalt with the Amex Business Platinum or Personal Platinum. Both cards pool MR points into the same account, letting you combine Cobalt's 5x earning with Platinum's higher welcome bonus (up to 100,000 MR points). One account, maximum points.

Travel Insurance Coverage

For a $12.99/month card, the Cobalt punches above its weight on insurance:

  • Travel accident insurance: Up to $500,000 coverage when you charge the full fare to your Cobalt
  • Flight and baggage delay: Covers meals and accommodation when delays exceed 4 hours
  • Hotel burglary insurance: Covers belongings stolen from your hotel room
  • No medical insurance: The Cobalt does not include travel medical insurance — a notable gap. Supplement with a standalone travel medical policy or a card like the TD Aeroplan Infinite, which does include emergency medical.

Who Should NOT Get the Cobalt

The Cobalt isn't perfect for everyone:

  • Low food spenders: If you spend less than $400/month on food and dining, the math gets tighter. You'd still likely break even, but the margin shrinks.
  • Costco-primary shoppers: Costco does not accept American Express cards in Canada. If Costco is your main grocery source, the Cobalt is useless there — consider the Costco Mastercard instead for grocery spending.
  • Points simplicity seekers: MR points require more management than a simple cash back card. If you just want to redeem points automatically without thinking about transfer partners, a cash back card may suit you better.

2025 Card Comparisons

Amex Cobalt vs. Scotiabank Gold Amex

The Scotiabank Gold Amex earns 6x on groceries (at Sobeys-bannered stores only), 5x at other grocery/dining, and includes travel medical insurance — a meaningful advantage. However, Scene+ points are less flexible than Amex MR points. For pure flight redemption value, Cobalt's MR→Aeroplan path wins. For simplicity and medical coverage, consider Scotiabank Gold Amex.

Amex Cobalt vs. TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite

These cards complement each other rather than compete. The TD Aeroplan earns directly in Aeroplan miles, includes travel medical insurance, and provides first checked bag free on Air Canada. The Cobalt earns more points faster. Hold both if you can — use Cobalt for daily spending and TD Aeroplan for Air Canada purchases.
